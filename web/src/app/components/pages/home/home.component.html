OUR MISSION

We are a group of four students at Virginia Commonwealth University. This installation was part of a semester long project for one of our courses. Our instructions were to interrupt a space. How we interpretted that and executed it were left completely open ended. My teammates and I all took interest to this topic of self love and how we could help people, especially students, value themselves more. Thus the Love Myself Project was born. This website is a look into not only the final product of our installation but also all of the process leading up to it. Here we have laid out all of our research, campus partners, and initial goals for the project.

The Love Myself Project hopes to raise awareness for and encourage individuals to love themselves. Self-hate and self-depreciation is rampant in online communities; young people are struggling with a sense of self-worth. LMP aims to change that through a brief but impactful week long engagement on the Virginia Commonwealth University campus, showing love and giving hope.

In the end we felt as though the essence of this project was a success. There were parts that we felt could have had more improvement had we had the time such as the social media accounts, and participations from campus organizations. The turn out for the installation and those who participated in it was beyond successful in our eyes, we reached over 100 people a day. It is hard to measure the full impact that we made although people's reactions were encouraging. If this project were to continue it would be best to have this become a reoccurring installation that came back every month or every few months.

The steps that we had to go through with the VCU administration was a little more intense than we expected. We sometimes found it difficult to meet with people, and eventually just began showing up and reaching out to everyone that we could in order to schedule meetings. If we could change anything it would be the marketing leading up to the event. We should have been marketing long before and finding other student organizations to participate with us.
